## Verified product truths

- Swipe right to keep a photo or video.
- Swipe left to mark clutter for deletion.
- Work month by month or filter by videos, screenshots, albums, and recents.
- Review what is marked before completing deletion.
- The listing screenshot explains that deleted items remain recoverable for 30 days through the device’s normal recovery flow.
- Track cleanup progress and storage reclaimed.
- No account is required.
- Photo-library sorting and deletion happen on-device; the listing says the library is not uploaded or stored by KeepYeet.
- Duplicate cleanup is an owner-confirmed launch feature that was not yet documented in the public App Store listing during this research pass. Final deletion remains user-controlled. No source supports AI “best shot” selection or video compression claims.

## Public privacy disclosure

Apple’s public privacy label says the developer may handle:

- Data used to track you: identifiers.
- Data linked to you: purchases, location, identifiers, and usage data.
- Data not linked to you: diagnostics.

Copy guardrail: “photos stay on-device” and “no account required” are supported. “No tracking,” “no data collected,” and “100% private” are not supported by the current privacy label and must not be used.

## Keyword research method

Target market: English-language US search, with platform-neutral wording suitable for the Android launch.

Research combined:

- Exact product relevance and likely download intent.
- Language repeated by current category leaders and live search results.
- Problem-aware queries people use before they know a product name.
- SERP competition from current cleaner-app landing pages and App Store results.
- Conversion fit: manual control, swipe speed, month-by-month cleanup, storage relief, and on-device photo handling.

No paid keyword-volume tool or Search Console history was available. The scores below are directional research scores, not measured monthly searches. “Demand” and “difficulty” use a 1–100 relative scale. Opportunity follows `(demand × .4) + ((100 − difficulty) × .3) + (relevance × .3)`.

## Competitor and SERP observations

Current search results consistently frame the category around “photo cleaner,” “camera roll,” “free up space,” and swipe control. Representative results include [Clever Cleaner](https://www.cleverfiles.com/clever-cleaner/), [Vacuu](https://vacuu.app/), [Pikondo](https://pikondo.app/), [Photo Cleaner](https://www.photocleaner.io/), [PhotoHelp](https://photohelp.app/), and [Swipe Photo Cleaner](https://www.swipephotocleaner.com/).

KeepYeet’s strongest conversion position is **fast, deliberate, game-like cleanup with the user in control**. Duplicate cleanup expands the commercial-keyword opportunity, while the memorable “Keep / Yeet” language, month-by-month workflow, review-before-delete reassurance, and simple swipe interaction should lead.
