# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** July 17, 2026

---

## Introduction and Scope

This Privacy Policy explains how **Omer Yom Tov (KeepYeet)** ("KeepYeet," "we," "us," or "our") handles information in connection with the KeepYeet mobile application, available on iOS and Android (the **"App"**). This policy applies only to the App.

KeepYeet helps you review, organise, and clean your photo or media library. The App does not require an account, registration, or login, and we do not ask you to provide a name, phone number, or email address to use it. If you contact us, we receive the information you choose to include in your message.

KeepYeet does not operate a backend that receives or stores your photos or videos. Media processing and sorting take place on your device. Limited technical, analytics, advertising, attribution, notification, and purchase data may be processed by the third-party services described below.

For privacy questions or requests, contact us at [omer@keepyeet.app](mailto:omer@keepyeet.app).

## Information We Handle

### Photos, Videos, and Local App Data

The App needs permission to access the photos and videos you allow it to access so that you can review, sort, share, and delete them.

- KeepYeet does not upload your photo or video library to a KeepYeet-operated server.
- The App may read media metadata on your device, such as an asset identifier, media type, dimensions, creation date, duration, file size, and favourite status.
- On Android, the App may create cached thumbnail copies to display media efficiently. These thumbnails remain on the device and are subject to App-cache, device-storage, and uninstall behaviour.
- Sorting decisions, deletion selections, session history, progress, statistics, onboarding responses, usage and advertising-related records, and preferences may be stored locally on your device.
- The App's **Start Fresh** control clears session and sorting progress, but it does not necessarily clear every local preference, SDK record, cached file, or record held by a third-party provider. Other local App data generally remains until the App's data is cleared through device controls or the App is uninstalled, subject to your operating system's storage and backup behaviour.
- When you confirm a deletion, iOS may move the item to **Recently Deleted**, while Android may use its system trash or deletion flow. The operating system controls recovery and permanent deletion.
- If you choose to share a photo or video, your operating system sends the selected item to the destination you choose. This user-initiated sharing is not an upload to a KeepYeet backend.

You can change the App's media permissions in your device settings.

### Onboarding and Product-Usage Information

The App may collect onboarding responses and product-usage information to understand how the App is used and improve the experience. This may include:

- Your general cleanup goals, camera-roll situation, motivation, estimated library size, and referral source;
- An approximate age-range response, including a `13–17` option, rather than your exact birth date;
- Screens viewed, buttons tapped, session activity, sorting and deletion aggregates, media counts, storage amounts, and feature interactions;
- App version, build number, platform, permission status, subscription status, and time since installation.

Onboarding responses may be stored locally and associated with a pseudonymous analytics identifier. PostHog session replay is disabled; KeepYeet does not record replays of your screen through PostHog.

### Technical, Diagnostic, and Identifier Data

Our service providers may automatically receive limited technical information, including:

- IP address and approximate or coarse location inferred from network information;
- Device type, operating system and version, language, time zone, App version, and build information;
- Advertising identifiers where available and permitted, plus pseudonymous identifiers created by individual SDKs;
- App launches, session information, performance information, crash or error data, and other diagnostics;
- Notification permission status, push token, and notification subscription identifier.

KeepYeet does not maintain a registered-user profile. Provider-generated identifiers may nevertheless be linked between certain services for attribution, purchase measurement, entitlement management, or notification support.

### Purchases and Subscriptions

Purchases are processed by Apple's App Store or Google Play, with RevenueCat supporting product availability, purchases, subscriptions, trials, entitlements, and restore functionality. We do not receive or store your full payment-card details.

Apple, Google, and RevenueCat may process transaction identifiers, product information, purchase history, subscription or trial status, entitlement status, store account purchase metadata, and related device or SDK identifiers. You can manage subscriptions and purchases through the applicable App Store or Google Play settings.

### Advertising and Attribution

The App may offer or display in-app advertising, including advertising associated with App functionality or additional access when presented. How advertising is presented may vary based on the current App version, eligibility, location, choices, device settings, and provider configuration.

Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) may display privacy choices where required or configured. On iOS, Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) setting controls access to certain tracking identifiers. On Android, advertising and privacy controls are available through the device and Google settings. The availability and type of advertising, and the information used for it, may depend on your location, choices, device settings, and provider configuration. Denying ATT does not by itself guarantee that every advertisement will be non-personalized.

AdMob may process advertising identifiers, IP address, inferred coarse location, ad interactions, impression information, diagnostic information, and advertising data. Ad lifecycle and revenue information, such as ad format, placement, impression identifier, ad network, revenue, currency, and errors, may also be reported to RevenueCat for measurement.

AppsFlyer processes limited install, session, campaign, attribution, and deep-link information. Its pseudonymous identifier may be provided to RevenueCat for attribution and purchase measurement.

### Notifications

The App uses OneSignal to support notification delivery through Apple Push Notification Service (APNs) on iOS and Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) on Android. When OneSignal is initialized, it may process a notification subscription identifier, device and App information, permission status, and session information before you decide whether to allow notifications. If you allow notifications, it may also process a push token and deliver notifications to your device.

KeepYeet may associate the pseudonymous RevenueCat App User ID with OneSignal and may provide OneSignal with subscription status and an entitlement-expiration timestamp to support subscription-related notification timing. A OneSignal identifier may also be provided to RevenueCat. You can disable notifications in your device settings.

### Contact Information

If you contact us by email, we receive your email address, message, and any other information you choose to provide. We use it to respond, provide support, handle privacy requests, and maintain necessary records.

## Retention

Retention depends on the type of information and where it is stored:

- Session and sorting progress can be cleared using **Start Fresh**. Other local App data, SDK records, and cached files remain subject to device settings, provider controls, operating-system storage and backup behaviour, and uninstall behaviour as described above.
- Contact and support correspondence is retained for as long as reasonably necessary to respond, maintain appropriate records, resolve disputes, and comply with legal obligations.
- Purchase and entitlement information is retained while needed to verify access, manage or restore purchases, prevent fraud, maintain transaction records, resolve disputes, and meet accounting or legal obligations, subject also to the policies of Apple, Google, and RevenueCat.
- Product analytics and attribution information is retained for the configured measurement period needed to analyse usage trends, campaign attribution, reliability, and performance, after which the provider may delete or aggregate it according to the applicable configuration and privacy notice.
- Notification information is retained while a notification subscription remains active or while needed to maintain subscription-related notification status, and afterward according to OneSignal, Apple, and Google's deletion and retention processes.
- Advertising information is retained according to the applicable provider configuration and for periods needed for ad delivery, measurement, billing, fraud prevention, dispute handling, and legal obligations.

We do not retain your photo or video library on a KeepYeet-operated server.
